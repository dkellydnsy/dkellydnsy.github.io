Rattus Maximus (Rat 101)

Rat 101 content below contains facts/trivia about NYC Rats.

  • The Norwegian Rat (Species: Rattus norvegicus) is the most common rat in NYC.

  • The NYC Brown (and Black) Rats are not considered to be indidigenous to NYC (nor North America for that matter).

  • NYC Rats are brown/grey rodents with a body lenght of of 10in (25cm) long with a similar length for their tails.

  • Males NYC Brown Rats weigh about 12oz (350g) which is about 40% heavier than female Brown Rats weigh: 9 oz (250g).

  • NYC's Brown Rats have excellent acute hearing and are sensitive to ultrasound.
    NYC's Brown Rats also posess a very highly developed olifactory sense (accute sense of smell).

  • The NYC Brown Rats have extremely poor vision.
    Therefore Rats primarily rely on their sense of smell to navigate within their environment;
    along with utilizing their ultra-sensitivity to sounds to complement their powerful olifactory
    senses to make up for their very very poor eyesight.

  • NYC Brown Rats are considered to be true omnivores and will eat almost anything.
    They are especially fond of cereals and grains.
